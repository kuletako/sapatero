Your Digital Measure Guide

Every pair of Sapatero shoes begins with a precise understanding of the wearer. Follow our artisanal measurement protocol to provide the blueprints for your handcrafted legacy.

The Kit You'll Need

description

Two sheets of A4 or larger, ensuring your entire foot fits comfortably on the surface.

straighten

Soft Tape & Ruler

A flexible measuring tape for circumference and a rigid ruler for length and width.

A Four-Step Protocol

Precision is the soul of bespoke footwear. Please follow these steps carefully, ideally wearing the socks you intend to wear with your shoes.

01 / ARCHIVE THE SILHOUETTE

Trace Your Foot

Place your foot firmly on the paper. Keeping your weight distributed evenly, trace the entire outline of your foot. Hold the pencil perpendicular to the paper—do not tilt it inward or outward, as this will skew the measurements.

  • check_circle Stand on a hard floor, not carpet.
  • check_circle A second person tracing for you is highly recommended.
02 / VERTICAL AXIS

Measure Length

Using your ruler, measure the distance from the very back of the heel to the tip of your longest toe. This measurement defines the primary last size. Measure in millimeters for the highest level of accuracy.

Pro Tip: Your left and right feet are rarely identical. Always measure both and provide the larger value.

03 / WIDTH & VOLUME

Measure Width

Identify the widest points of your foot (the ball). Measure across the flat tracing, then use the soft tape to measure the circumference around the same points.

04 / THE ARCH PROFILE

Arch Height

Wet your foot and step onto a piece of dry paper. The shape of the imprint reveals your arch height—essential for proper internal support and lasting comfort.

Ready to Submit?

Once you have your figures, upload them to our Bespoke Portal. Our master cordwainers will review your data before starting the last-making process.
